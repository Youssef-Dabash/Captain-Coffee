Coffee components of nutrients

Coffee contains some nutrients that are beneficial to health, including the following

A rich source of antioxidants

Coffee contains polyphenol compounds, which are antioxidants that help reduce free radicals, which cause damage to the body's cells, and may lead to inflammation associated with metabolic syndrome, which includes type 2 diabetes and obesity.

A good source of vitamins and minerals

Coffee contains many vitamins and minerals, such as magnesium, potassium, niacin, choline, and others

There are types of coffee, including the following
Arabic coffee

CIn the Middle East, coffee is brewed and prepared with the addition of several spices, such as cardamom, cinnamon, saffron, and ginger, to balance the bitter flavor of coffee. Dates and fruits are also served with Arabic coffee. It has also been noted that Arabic coffee prepared with cardamom or cloves provides antioxidant activity.

Turkish coffee

Turkish coffee is one of the methods of preparing coffee in the Middle East and European countries, such as Turkey, Iran, and Greece. It is more concentrated compared to other types of coffee, so it can provide many health benefits, due to its containing larger quantities of nutrients, such as chlorogenic acid, which belongs to the antioxidant polyphenol compounds.

The discovery of coffee

The discovery of coffee is believed to have been discovered by the Oromo people of Ethiopia, who were the first to discover and recognize the stimulating effects of the coffee bean plant. No direct evidence has been found to reveal the location of coffee in the world, or to reveal who used it as a stimulant or not even before the 17th century. The earliest reliable evidence of either coffee or coffee tree drinking appears in the 15th century, in the Sufi monasteries of Yemen in southern Arabia. Coffee was taken from Ethiopia to Yemen, Egypt and the Arabian Peninsula, and soon by the 15th century had reached Armenia, Persia and Turkey. It was written from the Islamic world, to Italy, then to Malaysia, Indonesia and the Americas.
